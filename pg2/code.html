3. Entanglement Swapping Protocols

Sec. III — Theoretical Frameworks

The core mechanism of entanglement swapping relies on the projection of two independent photons onto a Bell state. If Alice and Bob share an entangled pair $(A, B)$, and Bob and Charlie share another pair $(C, D)$, a joint Bell-state measurement (BSM) by Bob on particles $B$ and $C$ will instantly project particles $A$ and $D$ into an entangled state, despite them never having interacted.

This mechanism forms the backbone of quantum repeaters, essential for long-distance quantum key distribution (QKD). The fidelity of the final state $F_{AD}$ is fundamentally bounded by the initial purities of the pairs and the efficiency of the BSM setup. We formalize this bound as:

$F_{AD} \le F_{AB} \cdot F_{CD} + \frac{1}{3}(1 - F_{AB})(1 - F_{CD})$
